About Dr. Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, popularly known as the Missile Man of India, was a renowned Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, in 1931, Kalam rose from humble beginnings to become a key figure in India's defense and space research sectors.

He worked with organizations such as DRDO and ISRO, where he led the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. His work earned him a reputation as a brilliant scientist and a dedicated public servant. Despite his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam remained a humble man, devoted to education and inspiring youth.

Milestones of a Legend

Scientific Career

Played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests and development of SLV-III, India’s first satellite launch vehicle.

President of India

Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the 'People’s President' for his approachability and vision.

Inspiration for Youth

Dr. Kalam dedicated his post-presidency life to teaching, writing, and motivating students across India with his visionary ideas.

Books by Dr. Kalam

  • Wings of Fire – An autobiography of India's "Missile Man".
  • Ignited Minds – A call to India's youth to awaken and rise.
  • India 2020 – A vision for transforming India into a developed nation.
  • My Journey – Reflections of a scientist's life.
